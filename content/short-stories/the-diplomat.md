The room smells like roasted regret.

The acrid cigarette smoke is continuing to fill my lungs. Another bout of nausea hits me like a brick wall. I want to cough, the compulsion just gets worse.

But I have to get this deal done... and I better get the transfer I was promised for it, or else there’s going to be hell to pay.

I continue looking at the guy sitting opposite of me - my personal indoor chimney.

He really does remind me of a rat... not only because of the pear-shaped figure slumped in the chair, and distinct facial features, but also - or perhaps, even more so - because of that occasional look he shows deep in his eyes.

Thankfully, I don’t have to see them while he’s hunched over the conference table, looking over the documents...

...for the billionth time...

It’s really in everyone’s interests I get that transfer back home. 

When you think about it, foreign placement doesn’t even make any sense for someone like me... in fact, I’m pretty sure I’m the only one out of my peers who’s used like this. I do get it from a purely pragmatic standpoint, but it’s still weird. What if-...

patap, cigarette ash hit the glass tabletop, sprinkles flying off in all directions.

“Shit!”, the guy instinctively swiped it with his hand, which only made it worse, smearing ash all over the glass and the hand.

But after that momentary distress, he froze for just half a second, and then looked up at me with a sudden cavalier attitude, shrugging, “Heh, welp~... I guess at least today the janitors will earn their pay”

What an ass.

Also, you clearly panicked just now, what’s the use of this facade at this point?

“Haha”, I hide any signs my chuckle is fake, while trying to avoid accidentally looking directly at the cigarette in his fingers, or the thin smoke rising from it’s end.

And he’s back to reading the proposed terms of the agreement... again... 

Come on, man! This was supposed to be a short meeting... and it’s not like you’re the one who will be signing it either way... you don’t even have a say in altering anything that’s in there, that’s your superior who you’re just supposed to confirm the contents. 

A bead of sweat is coming down the side of his face... he’s clearly distressed... over dropping ash on the table. There also was no tabletop ashtray set up here, he has to use a pocket ashtray he brought with him...  

Yep, this guy really has the lowest standing here. And he’s the one they send out for this meeting... just great.

Sure, I admit that Junior Assistant to the Embassy Liaison Officer isn’t a big role... but it’s not like I’m from some minor country... if anything, they’re the ones who should be on their toes... is it my age? No need to pay respects to someone so young? 

He puffs out another cloud of smoke. So annoying.

Motherf-...

A bright smile, “Of course, this is just the rough outline proposal. The rest is to be negotiated. Does that mean we agree on broader issues?”

Laughter fills the room, as he shakes his head.
