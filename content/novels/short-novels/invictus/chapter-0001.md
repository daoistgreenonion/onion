title: "In the darkness..."
---

“Your father’s dead.”'

A harsh, abrasive voice spat out those words into a thin strip of flickering light that opened up with a metal screech in the solid wooden door.

A smirk of the thinly bearded lips now visible through the opening, and the gleeful tone in the voice betrayed the speaker’s intentions.

Hearing nothing in reply, the smirk became a scowl, “Hey! You hear me?! Hey!!!”, a loud banging of a fist on the wooden door echoed through the stale, slightly humid, cold air.

“Hey! Fucker! Did you hear what I just told you?! Your father’s dead! His head got chopped off just a while ago! And you’re next!” the voice taunted again while banging on the wood of the door with an open palm.

“Jeez man, he’s just a kid-...”, another voice spoke with an uncomfortable intonation.

But it was interrupted by the first voice as the bearded mouth visible through the opening in the door turned to the side, “Just a kid?”

There was incredulousness in that hushed tone that contrasted with how the man spoke before.

What came next was an outburst full of stifled, seething anger, as the voice instantly rose to a roar: “Just a kid?!?! That ‘thing’ killed Rasmel and Tom right before my eyes… my best friends… that son of a bitch butchered them like fucking cattle!!!”

“...”, the other voice was silenced.

After seemingly staring down the other one, the owner of the bearded mouth turned to the opening again, “Haha, though I guess, it's your old man who died like cattle… My friends died in battle like the warriors they were. Your father, on the other hand…”

The man made a pause as if picturing the scene in his mind.

“Helpless and on his knees, stripped of his shiny armor and fancy clothes… and squealing like a pig that is dragged away to be slaughtered…”, these words were spoken with clear enjoyment.

And then the tone of the voice shifted, as if the speaker suddenly remembered something, “Oh yeah… it was red… funny, but I actually was surprised, you nobles bleed red too after all! Blue blood my ass! Why would you tell such an obvious lie?”

There was a hint of genuine frustration in the last sentence, as if the speaker was annoyed by being swindled. And yet, despite there being no reply the man continued to have a ‘conversation’.

“You should've seen how much red he splashed around! Hahaha\~!!! ah~ What a sight\~!”, the laughter ended on a high note, with a gleeful falling off after, as the voice sounded like someone was recalling fond memories, and then once more had an idea pop up spontaneously, “Maybe yours is blue? Wouldn’t be surprised though… Aren’t you going to ask when is it? Your execution?”

“...”, however, silence is all that came back to him yet again.

“Will it be tomorrow? Or the day after? Aren’t you curious? I bet you are! Want me to tell ya?”, the man did not give up trying to get a reaction, leaving a small dramatic pause after each question or phrase, but it was all futile. His frustration also became more and more obvious throughout his talk.

“W-what are you doing, Birk?”, the second voice spoke up with exasperation as metallic sounds of a key being inserted into a lock made its way into the darkness of the room the man apparently named Birk was shouting into.

“It’s too quiet, who knows, he might’ve croaked in there”, Birk replied while turning the lock.

“You can’t! H-he’s an Aether Knight!”, the other one now had a tinge of panic in his voice.

“Tsk, do I look like an idiot?! I told you I saw him fight, Finn. Of course I wouldn’t go in there if he was just sitting there with no restraints! The chains he’s bound by are from Selis alloy, he can’t use Aether now even if he had Aether gemstones, which he doesn’t”, Birk had a grumbling, annoyed tone of voice now, which quickly changed into a mocking one, “Besides, what Aether Knight? Sir Alan is an Aether Knight, this little shit is barely an Aether user” 

“Th-there’s a difference?”

“Hah! Ii\~idiot! Of course there is a difference! They… uh… an Aether Knight is… well…  they’re just different, got it?”

“I-I see-...”, the scrawny man, called Finn, whose silhouette appeared in the opening of the creaking door first, nodded with a somewhat dimwitted expression, and then, while still finishing to speak, unconsciously turned his head to the widening gap of darkness behind the creaking door. 

It was as if a tiny bit at the very end of his speech was cut off, “Hiiieeek!!!”, he shrieked while falling back onto his butt.

“Wha-...”, the more robustly built one with a thinned out short beard that went just a little below his chin, Birk, was the one opening the door, and thus appeared in the door frame after. He quickly turned to look at what spooked his partner so much, and froze in place, as a chill went up his spine, making the hair on the back of his head stand up.

Two weakly glowing yellowish eyes, resembling that of a lion, coldly stared at the pair from the darkness. That quiet and calm, and yet somewhat animal-like gaze seemed to make the blood in their veins curdle.

But as the eyes of the two adapted to the gloomy lighting of the cell, the torch on the wall behind them shining a bit of light into its darkness, they could see that the young boy who looked to be no older than 17 was securely restrained by a rather short chain bolted into the center of the tiny cell room floor on one end, and a solid metal restraint that bound his wrists together on the other.

If he got up, he could not even stand straight, as the chain length would force him to heavily lean forward.

The prisoner was barefoot, stripped to only worn out black pants, exposing a young, but already well-built upper body. Not bulky, but with well defined, lean muscles built for endurance, with older and newer scars criss-crossing the toughened skin. 

He was half-sitting half-lying back, leaning his head and upper back on the cellar wall made of cut stone behind him. His short black hair was ruffled on the left side, and dried blood covered half of his face under it. Apart from several large bruises coloring his torso, there was also a rather large gash on his right side abdomen, the wound seemingly left untended for quite some time, but not bleeding at this moment, although there was a dried blood stain stretching below it into a small puddle under him. 

“Heh! See? Nothing to fear”, Birk spoke with a slightly nervous snort, a mocking expression reappearing on his face.

His companion gulped, unable to answer and only nodded, though there wasn’t much confidence in that nod.

“You’re not dead after all, little shit… why so quiet then? You didn’t hear me before?”, Birk spoke while narrowing his eyes. There was still a bit of fear in his eyes as he looked at this young kid, memories of seeing him in battle a few days before were still fresh in his mind. But that fear was quickly turning into confidence, seeing how weakened the youth looked.

There were visible signs of starvation and fatigue, as well as the feverishness from an undressed wound left to fester. The rotten, moldy air of the inside of the cell, even colder and more humid than outside the door, also wasn’t facilitating recovery. Even if he was an Aether user, his body was still too young and clearly was struggling to endure these conditions.

The look in Birk’s eyes changed, as his fear now turned not just into confidence, but past that point, into irritation. The calmness of the prisoner, despite the difference in their current situation, irked him.

“What’s with that look? You still think you’re some big shot?!”, Birk said as Finn finally got up from the floor behind him.

“Hey… come on, you checked that he’s not dead. Let’s lock up the door and go”, the scrawny guy nervously looked at the wounded and weakened youth and then looked around to check if someone saw them opening up the cell when they were not supposed to.

“Hang on, I gotta teach this arrogant bastard some humility”, Birk felt more and more angry at the lack of any emotion, let alone a response, in the prisoner’s expression and overall demeanor.

Seeing the one who so easily killed normal soldiers like him in battle, so weakened, so vulnerable, gave him a sudden rush of feeling powerful. But at the same time, there wasn’t a satisfactory feeling, as the youth showed no despair or fear.

It was as if something dark welled up from deep inside Birk, a side of himself he didn’t know, and a sudden impulse to assert his newfound power over such a strong foe, to break that annoying willpower he himself lacked, pushed him to take that first, slightly wavering step, into the dark cell.

“H-hey! Come o\~n man! Let’s just go\~!”, his companion’s voice broke from nervousness and outright fear, resulting in a rather pathetic sound.

“Relax, it’ll be over quick. I’ll just smack some sense into him and we can go”, the man spoke as his second step was already full of confidence. His hand in leather glove opening and closing in anticipation of hitting the one he wished to see despair. He was already imagining how after a few hits the prisoner’s demeanor would change, an exhilarating rush taking over his thinking.

“This feels wrong man, we shouldn’t be…”, the scrawny man continued in a whiny tone, until…

“Shut up, Finn, just shut up!”, Birk snapped and turned a bit to glare and shout at the scrawny companion behind him, momentarily losing track of where he was stepping toward. However, he suddenly saw abject horror distorting Finn’s face, as the metallic sound of a chain reached his ear, while something moved in the corner of his sight, something in front of him.

He jerked his head to look forward and froze, shivers going through his body, as those weakly glowing eyes were right in front of him now, looking up at him from under eyebrows as the prisoner was standing with a hunched back, the chain firmly holding his wrists down.

“Heh! Wha-...”, Birk’s frozen expression started to turn into a smirk as he realized that he wasn’t in danger because of the chain, but the bound youth suddenly lunged at him.

“B-Birk!!!”, Finn cried out to his companion, but could only watch as the latter fell backward, holding onto his neck as the cell floor under him quickly turned into a puddle of dark liquid.

“Kk-...! khuk-...!”, Birk’s eyes bulged as he held onto his neck with both hands, trying to keep the blood from gushing out of his throat that was missing a torn out chunk, as gurgling and wheezing sounds filled the tiny cell room.

Finn, still standing beyond the door, gripped his head with both hands and slowly started lowering his posture, or rather, his knees gradually gave out as he looked on helplessly at the bleeding out Birk.

“Pthoo”, the youth spat to the side, a meaty chunk of torn out throat landing on the floor with a ‘splat’ and blood splattering around it, fresh blood around his mouth contrasting the dried one on the side of his face, adding in a slightly raspy from fatigue and bloodloss voice, “Should’ve grown out that beard instead of half-assing it.”
